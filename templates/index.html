What is
DRUG REPURPOSING?

Drug repurposing, also known as drug repositioning or reprofiling, is the process of identifying new uses for existing drugs that are already approved for other indications. Instead of developing entirely new drugs, researchers and pharmaceutical companies explore the therapeutic potential of existing drugs for different diseases or conditions. This approach offers several advantages, including faster development timelines, lower development costs, and reduced risks compared to developing entirely newly compounds.

Drug Repurposing
